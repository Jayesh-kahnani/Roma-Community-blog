Unveiling the Romani Community

The Great Romani History

Prasanna Nov 22 • 3 min read

The people were actually “The Roma” but they are known to the world as “The Gypsies”. This community is well known to be exiled, discriminated against, separated and enslaved almost in every region they go. There is even a gypsy proverb that goes like - “Bury me standing up for, I've been on my knees all my life.” This proverb alone explains how excluded the gypsies were in European societies since they entered that land. There are actually many literary works that describe the roma community. They are generally known to be dark coloured, moving from town to town, telling fortunes and playing hypnotic tunes on their violins along with their carts.

Yale strom who was a filmmaker and a musician says that - “Fortune tellers”, “ Palm readers”, “ crystal ball”, “ violinists”, “dancers”, “long dresses" are some of the positive terms that are told by children when they are asked about the gypsies. The negatives are “untrustworthy”, “stealing children”, “promiscuous”. All the information that we get from people about gypsies is from newspaper articles, tv series, movie characters or from traditional gypsy folklore only. The actual real gypsies are very little known. Gordon Boswell, a gypsy, seems to be very proud of his race, his background, and his people and would say the same when someone asks “Are you a gypsy?”. There are almost 8 to 12 million Romani people in almost every continent of the world. This distinct group of people settled in different places in different communities and often integrated into the general population. No matter how many different kinds of gypsies exist, they all have a common origin point and are ultimately connected to the same heritage and are related to each other. Generally, most of the romanies can recognise another romani anytime, anywhere. Most of the romanies are dark skinned and have dark eyes and black hair. This nomadic community entered Europe in the 14th century and by that time, nobody knew their homeland, their culture nor their country. But they continued to maintain their identity by living in close bonds and protecting their community along with their common language called “Romani”. The superstitions and myths around the romani people are because of their secretive nature. They left no records but just passed their culture and identity onto the future generations. That's why most of the information about them in literature and movies is from outsiders and they were viewed with misconceptions by non gypsies who were referred to as “Gadje”.

How were gypsies treated from historical times..?

The romanies were treated as strangers in every country they entered and were even hunted like animals in some places in the mid 19th century. During the second world war, they were targeted by Hitler and were killed in mass numbers in his death camps. In the United States, gypsies would hide their race as they were afraid of revealing their true identity and live alongside the Americans in the 20th century. In some regions of the world, it was even legal to kill romanies and to enslave them. In Europe, they were not even allowed to take water from wells. From historical times, there is no single case that they were not discriminated against but today they are survivors and know how to survive as a group.

Where exactly do they originate from..?
The answer to this question would be traced from their musical evidence. They were more associated with their contribution to music. Their language in music gives a sense of longing for tunes and words which further leads our thoughts to identify the path of their departure. So their language is basically related to Sanskrit which has its roots in India. What is interesting is that the romani language is similar to hindi, punjabi and other north Indian languages. It is also proven by anthropological blood tests that the romani people resemble the warrior classes of India. The question “where did gypsies originate from?” has witnessed many debates and controversies and finally it is believed that they started their migration from North western india. These people are found in almost every continent across the globe including the regions of Turkey, Middle east. How did they enter Europe? While they began from India, they moved towards the Caspian sea and crossed caucasus mountains and reached the Byzantine empire. Along with their visits to different countries and regions, many new words have been added to their language. In the Byzantine empire, they had lived for almost 100 years. They were skilled in metal works there. There were actually records that describe gypsies as fortune tellers, herbal healers and entertainers also. After the fall of the Byzantine empire, they shifted their way to Europe. Some gypsies escaped from the feudal difficulties by the Turks in eastern europe. When they moved to Europe, people started to assume them as travelers from Egypt and that's how they got the name “Gypsies”. The gypsies currently make up almost 14 percent of the population of Europe. Many legislations or laws against them were made in eastern europe back then. The slavery treatment and exclusion they faced everywhere made them hide their identity to the outsiders which brought them a tagline that they were liars. The saddest part is that the gypsies had nowhere to go in the whole world and they were brutally persecuted everywhere they went. They lived as strangers and outsiders in every land. They were treated very badly because of the way they dressed, the way they looked and because of speaking the language that was never heard or known to anyone. The gypsies would move from one place that they don’t belong to another place that they still don’t belong to. The gypsy community has a great history that was filled with many superstitious beliefs, false ideas and a bitter past.
